I love doing competitions because of their diverse learning and collaborative opportunities under time constraints. Here I provide information on some of the competitions I've participated in.

Pacific Rim Regional Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition -- March 2012

The Pacific Rim Regional Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition (CCDC) is a security defense competition lasting two days. Your team of eight is given a room of computers with an unknown state of security. The goal is to secure the provided systems, while efficiently performing general operational business tasks. The catch is that during the whole competition, trained security specialists are breaking into your systems and creating new and interesting security holes. The teams also are required to give security presentations, and write security policies. In the March 2012 competition, my team placed fourth of eleven.

Comap Mathematical Modeling Competition -- Feb. 2011/2012

The mathematical modeling competition (MMC) is an international four day competition where participants are given a real world scenario to clearly express and model in a mathematical way.

In 2011 we received Honorable Mention ( top 30% of 2.8 thousand teams ).
